Basic Reconnaissance Course - Instructor Handbook

Selection Process

Rules and Regulations:

  • Attendee MUST have a mic in order to attend
  • Attendee must act mature at all times (FORECON has zero tolerance for Immaturity)
  • Attendee must be divisionless
  • Attendee must not be blacklisted by FORECON and the Marine Corps
  • Attendee must respect the instructor at all times
  • Attendee must follow every order given by the instructor
  • Attendee may only speak when given permission to do so
  • Attendee may only go AFK when they were given permission by the instructor
  • Attendee must not complain about anything while attending the selection

VC check:

VC check is a must before you start the selection. Have the attendee join the tryout VC upon joining the game. Have the attendee say “I am, (Username), and I want to join FORECON” in VC. (Kick anyone who refuses to speak in VC, can’t use its mic or against the VC regulation out of the selection)

VC Regulation:

  • Must have a clear and loud mic

Physical Assessment

Physical Assessment is the first phase of BRC and is the only phase that contains physical training. It will be hosted on Training Base private server. Before you start, do let the attendee know that this phase will take up to 2 hours. If they can’t stay for 2 hours or are not willing to stay for 2 hours, dismiss them.

Jumping Jacks:

  • 400 JJ’s

Instructor will be having the attendee do 400 JJ’s without having them know how much they need to do.

Airfield Marathon:

  • 20 laps

Instructor will have attendees run around the airfield for 20 laps without letting them know how many laps they need to run.

FORECON has zero tolerance for immaturity and complains, dismiss anyone who acts immature or complains about the phase.

Classroom Session

For this phase, it will be hosted in VC. Instructors would have to use their microphone to explain everything instead of copy-pasting.

General information of Force Reconnaissance:

Force Reconnaissance (FORECON) is a United States Marine Corps deep reconnaissance unit which supplies military intelligence to the Marine Corps Headquarters. Force Reconnaissance provides direct action and deep reconnaissance during large-scale operations.

Rules and Regulations:

  • Every information within the Force Reconnaissance is classified; this includes but is not limited to operations, database, documents, text in the FORECON Discord server, and any announcement made by FORECON HQ. Leaking any classified information will result in a blacklist from both FORECON and Marine Corps, as well as notifying MACV regarding it.
  • As an Operator in Force Reconnaissance, you have an oath of silence. Meaning that while you're in game, you may only speak in team chat. If you are found not using team chat, you will be removed and blacklisted from FORECON. You may whisper to any Military Police Corps member or Officer only if needed.
  • As an Operator, you must obey every order given by your superiors.
  • As an Operator, you must remain disciplined, professional and mature at all times.
  • As an Operator, you must respect everyone within the Force Reconnaissance.
  • As an Operator, you must use your mic while attending events.
  • As an Operator, you must complete your weekly quota.
  • As an Operator, you must follow the Chain of Command.

Uniform Regulations:

  • Hair: Male operators must use completely shaved or closely-trimmed hair with natural colors. Female operators must use hair tied in a bun or ponytail without it clipping headwear.
  • Beard: Beard may only be used by Brigadier General or above.
  • Skin color: Realistic skin tone
  • Uniform: ERDL uniform with subdued FORECON patch, light or SF rig. Worn with ERDL boonie and facepaint.

Surgical with conscious patient:

  • Apply a tourniquet
  • O2 Therapy (Nasopharyngeal Airway, Non-rebreather, O2 tank)
  • Put the patient unconscious (Apply IV, give anesthetic)
  • Surgery (Scalpel, Person 1 click suction, Person 2 click Catheter, Ballon, 6-o prolene, Remove ballon, remove catheter, 4-0 prolene)
  • Remove tourniquet
  • Treat blood loss and injury
  • Remove O2
  • Apply epinephrine
  • Remove IV

Surgical with unconscious patient:

  • Apply a tourniquet
  • O2 Therapy (Endotracheal Tube, O2 tank)
  • -Surgery (Scalpel, Person 1 click suction, Person 2 click Catheter, Ballon, 6-o prolene, Remove ballon, remove catheter, 4-0 prolene)
  • Remove tourniquet
  • Treat blood loss and injury
  • Remove O2
  • Apply epinephrine
  • Remove IV

Surgical with critical patient:

  • Apply a tourniquet
  • O2 Therapy (Endotracheal Tube, O2 tank)
  • Revive patient (Chest compressions 3 times, defib)
  • Surgery (Scalpel, Person 1 suction, Person 2 clamp, Person 1 5-o prolene, 4-o nylon, scalpel, Person 1 click suction, Person 2 click Catheter, Ballon, 6-o prolene, Remove ballon, remove catheter, 4-0 prolene)
  • Remove tourniquet
  • Treat blood loss and injury
  • Remove O2
  • Apply epinephrine
  • Remove IV

Practice Operation:

Phase 4 needs at least 4 people to begin. Attendees must do everything themselves without the help of Instructors.

Role Assigning:

Instructor must assign a team leader for the team. The team leader will be in charge of the whole practice operation; instructors are not allowed to help at all.

Briefing:

Attendees must walk to the Urban Warfare Training Site and gather every intelligence they are able to get and report back to base. They are not allowed to get spotted at all while gathering information.

Practice Operation:

  • Attendees must be able to find a way to walk to the mission Area and gather intelligence.
  • The intelligence they've gathered must be correct.
  • Attendees must work as a team at all times.
  • They will only have 2 lives.
  • They will be counted as dead if they were spotted by the enemy.
